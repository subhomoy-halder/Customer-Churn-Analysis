# Customer Churn Analysis

## Overview

Customer churn is often discussed as a customer retention problem. However, the more important business question is:

**What is the business impact of churn?**

This project analyzes customer churn through a business lens, focusing not only on identifying churn patterns but also on understanding their impact on revenue, customer lifetime value, and retention strategy.

Using the IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset, the analysis was structured around key business questions commonly faced by leadership teams, customer success managers, and revenue stakeholders.

---

## Business Questions

This analysis aims to answer the following questions:

1. How much revenue is at risk due to churn?
2. Which customers represent the highest future value at risk?
3. Which churn reasons cost the business the most revenue?
4. Which contract types have the highest churn?
5. Which internet services experience the highest churn?
6. Which services are associated with lower churn?
7. Which cities generate the most customer value?

---

## Key Findings

The analysis uncovered several high-impact findings:

- Identified **$3.69M in revenue already lost** due to customer churn.

- Quantified **$7.76M in Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) lost**, highlighting the long-term financial impact of churn.

- Found that **Month-to-Month contracts accounted for 88.55% of churned customers**, making contract structure one of the strongest churn indicators.

- Discovered that **Fiber Optic customers generated 81.99% of total churned revenue**, representing the highest-risk customer segment.

- Revealed that the top three churn reasons:
  - Competitor Made Better Offer
  - Competitor Had Better Devices
  - Attitude of Support Person
 
  collectively accounted for **$1.96M in lost revenue**, representing nearly half of all churn-related revenue loss.

- Identified **446 high-risk customers** contributing approximately **$3.02M in revenue**, representing a significant retention opportunity.

- Found that customers subscribed to services such as **Online Security** and **Premium Tech Support** exhibited substantially lower churn rates compared to customers without those services.

These findings provide a foundation for prioritizing retention initiatives, improving customer experience, and reducing future revenue loss.

---

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

1. **Prioritize retention efforts for Fiber Optic customers** due to their disproportionate contribution to churned revenue.

2. **Develop targeted retention campaigns for Month-to-Month contract customers**, as they represent the largest share of churned customers.

3. **Investigate competitor-related churn drivers** and strengthen product offerings, pricing strategies, and customer value propositions.

4. **Improve customer support experience** to address service-related churn and reduce revenue loss associated with support interactions.

5. **Promote value-added services** such as Online Security and Premium Tech Support among high-risk customers to improve retention outcomes.

## Key Insights

### Revenue and CLTV Provide a More Complete View of Churn

Customer counts help quantify the scale of churn, but Revenue and Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV) provide a clearer picture of its financial impact.

Analyzing churn through these metrics helps identify not only how many customers are leaving, but also how much current and future revenue is being lost.

---

### Customer Value Is Not Distributed Evenly

A relatively small segment of customers can account for a disproportionately large share of revenue and future value at risk.

This highlights the importance of prioritizing retention efforts toward high-value customers rather than applying broad retention strategies across the entire customer base.

---

### Understanding Why Customers Leave Matters

Identifying churned customers is only the first step.

Analyzing churn reasons helps uncover the underlying drivers behind customer attrition, including:

* Competitive offers
* Product and service offerings
* Customer support experience
* Pricing concerns
* Service quality

These insights can support targeted retention initiatives and business improvements.

---

### Customer Behavior Can Signal Churn Risk

Customer behavior often provides stronger indicators of churn risk than customer demographics.

Factors such as:

* Contract type
* Service subscriptions
* Product adoption
* Internet service type

can reveal meaningful differences in churn behavior across customer segments.

---

### Analytics Should Drive Business Decisions

The most valuable outcome of churn analysis is not the dashboard itself, but the ability to support business decisions.

By connecting customer behavior to revenue and customer lifetime value, organizations can better understand:

* Where revenue is being lost
* Which customers require retention efforts
* Which products or services influence churn
* Where operational improvements can have the greatest impact
